Aim:

To determine the resistance of a given wire and to determine the specific resistance of the material of the wire. To verify the laws of series and parallel connections of resistance.

Apparatus:

Meter Bridge, Battery eleiminator, table galvanometer, high resistance, jockey, plug key and connecting wires.

Theory:

When the wheatstones bridge is balanced then \(\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S} \) where P, Q, R, and S are the resistances in the bridge. At the balance point the current through the galvanometer is zero. Meter bridge works on the principle of Wheatstones bridge.

Description:

Meter bridge consists of a uniform wire AB of one meter length, stretched on a wooden board. The two ends of the wire are connected to two L shaped copper strips. A horizontal copper strip is fixed between the L shaped strip so that two gaps G1 and G2 are formed as shown in the figure. Terminals are provided at the ends of the wire and at the ends of the gaps. These terminals are used for the connections. A horizontal scale is provided under the wire to measure the balancing length.

The two ends of the wire AB are connected to a battery E and a key K in series as shown. The wire of unknown resistance X and a resistance box are connected in the two gaps G1 and G2. The mid point P is connected to a Jockey J in series with a high resistance HR and a sensitive galvanometer G. There is a provision for the HR to exclude or to include in the circuit.

Procedure:

  1. To determine the unknown resistance X:

    The connections are made as shown in the circuit diagram. To check the connections, the jockey is pressed near A and then near B. If the opposite defections in the galvanometer, are observed the connections are correct. A known resistance R is introduced in the resistance box. The jockey is pressed on the wire at various points starting from A with the HR in the circuit. The defelctions go on decreasing. Locate a point where the galvanometer shows zero deflection. This is approximate balance point. The HR is excluded from the circuit and the correct balance point J is located. The balancing length L1 from the end A near the unknown resistance is measured. The remaining length of the wire is 100 - L1.

    The resistance x, R resistance of L1 length of wire and resistance of 100 - L1 length of wire from the resistances of wheatstones bridge.

    \( At \, Balance \, Point \frac{x}{R} = \frac{L_1}{100 - L_1} \)

    As the meter bridge wire is uniform, and as the resistance is proportional to the length, instead of ratio of resistances ratio of lengths of wire are taken.

    Knowing R and L, x the unknown resistance is calculated.

    The experiment is repeated with different values of R and the balancing lengths are determined. The reading are tabulated. The value of x is calculated in each case and the average value of x is calculated.

    Now the unkown resistance x and the resistance box are interchanged in the gaps. The experiment is repeated with different known values of R is in the resistance box. The balancing length is measure from the end which is nearer to unknown resistance. L2 is the balancing length.

    \( At \, Balance \, Point \frac{x}{R} = \frac{L_2}{100 - L_2} \)

    Reading are tabulated and the average value of x is calculated.

  2. To determine the specific resistance:

    The length L of the wire of unknown resistance is measured. The diamter of the wire at three different places on the wire are determined using the screw guage. The average diamter and hence the average radius r is calculated.

    \( s = \frac{X \pi r^2}{L} ohm \, cm\)

  3. To verify the law of series connection:

    The experiment is repeated keeping a wire of resistance R1 in gap 1 and the value R1 is determined. Now the wire of resistance R1 is replaced by another wire of resistance R2 in Gap 1 and its value is determined.

    Now R2 is replaced by the two wires connected in series in Gap 1 and the combined the value RS is determined.

    It is observed that R = R1 + R1 verifying the series combination of resitances.

  4. To verify the law of parallel combination:

    The above experiment is repeated with resistances R1 and R2 separately and their values are determined. Now the two resistances are connected in parallel and the effecttive value Rp is determined.

    It is observed that the above value agrees with the calculated value from the formula \( \frac{R_1R_2}{R_1 + R_2} \) verifying the law of parallel connection.

Precautions:

  • The resistance in the gaps are to be nearlyu equal.
  • The jockey should not be dragged on the wire kepping the circuit closed continuously.
  • Only small currents must be passed through the wire.
