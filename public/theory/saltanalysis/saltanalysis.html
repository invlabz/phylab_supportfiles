QUALITATIVE ANALYSIS

Analysy is of two types. They are 1. Qualitative analysis, 2. Quantitative analysis.

Qualitative analysis deals with the identification of constituent atoms or ions or molecules.

In qualitative inorganic salt analysis, we identify the cations and anions present in a given salt or mixture of salts. Every salt consists of two parts. They are basic radical or cation and actid radical or anion.

REACTIONS OF ANIONS

Reactions of Carbonate Ion CO3-2

Carbonates of sodium, potassium and ammonium salts are soluble in water.

S.No. Experiment Observation
1 Action of heat: A small quantity of the salt is taken in a dry test tube and strongly heated. All carbonates except those of sodium, potassium and barium decompose by producing carbondioxide.
\( CaCO_3 \rightarrow CaO + CO_2 \uparrow \)
a) A burning splinter is exposed to the gas obtained in the above reaction. The burning splinter is put out.
b) The gas obtained in the above reaction is passed into a test tube containing lime water. Lime water turning milky due to the formation of a precipitate of calcium carbonate
2 Action of dilute HCl: To a small quantity of the salt, a few drops of dilute HCl is added Carbondioxide is evolved with quick effervescence. The gas puts out burning splinter and turns lime water milky.
\( Na_2CO_3 + 2HCl \rightarrow 2NaCl + CO_2 \uparrow + H_2O\)
3 Action of BaCl2 To a small quantity of the salt, a few drops of BaCl2 is added A white precipitate of barium carbonate is formed which is soluble in dilute acids.
\( Na_2CO_3 + BaCl_2 \rightarrow BaCO_3 \downarrow + 2NaCl \)

Reactions of Chloride Ion Cl-

All chlorides dissolve in cold water. But the chlorides of silver, mercurous, cuprous and lead dissolve in hot water.

S.No. Experiment Observation
1 Action of heat: A small quantity of the salt is taken in a dry test tube and strongly heated. Colourless gases with pungent smell are evolved.
\( 2CuCl_2 \rightarrow 2CuCl + Cl_2 \uparrow \)
2 Action of Conc H2SO4: A small quantity of the salt is taken in a dry test tube and few ml of conc. H2SO4 is added Colourless gases with chlorine smell are evolved.
\( 2NaCl + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow Na_2SO_4 + 2HCl \)
A glass rod is dipped in ammonium hydroxide is exposed to the gases Dense white fumes of ammonium chloride are produced.
\( HCl + NH_3 \rightarrow NH_4Cl \)
3 Action of AgNO3: To the salt solution, silver nitrate solution is added. A curdy white precipitate of silver chloride is formed.
\( NaCl + AgNO_3 \rightarrow AgCl + NaNO_3 \)
Solubility of the precipitate: Add ammonia solution to the precipitate. Soluble in ammonia due to the formation of diamine silver Chloride, a complex
\( AgCl + 2NH_3 \rightarrow Ag(NH_3)_2Cl \)

Reactions of Nitrate Ion NO3-

S.No. Experiment Observation
1 Action of heat: A small quantity of the salt is heated gently in a dry test tube. Nitrate of alkali metals decompose to give oxygen. Heavy metallic nitrates give reddish brown vapours of NO2
\( 2NaNO_3 \rightarrow 2NaNo3 + O_2 \)
\( 2Pb(NO_3)_2 \rightarrow 2PbO + NO_2 + O_2 \)
\( NH_4NO_3 \rightarrow NO_2 + 2H_2O \)
2 Action of Con H2SO4: A small quantity of the salt in a dry test tube and add conentratated sulphuric acid and heat it. Reddish brown vapours of nitrogen dioxide are evolved.
\( NaNO_3 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow NaHSO_4 + HNO_3 \)
\( 4HNO_3 \rightarrow 4NO_2 + O_2 + 2H_2O \)
3 Brown ring test: A small quantity of the salt solution is taken in a test tube. Freshly prepared solution of ferrous sulphate is added in excess. Then the test tube is kept in an inclined position and concentrated sulphuric acid is added solowly drop by drop along the sides of the test tube. A brown ring is formed at the junction of the liquid layers nitroso ferrous sulphate is the composition of the brown ring.
\( 2NaNO_3 + H_2SO_4 \rightarrow NaHSO_4 + HNO_3 \)
\( 6FeSO_4 + 3H_2SO_4 + 2HNO_3 \rightarrow 3Fe2(SO_4)_3 + 2NO + 4H_2O \)
\( NO + FeSO_4 \rightarrow FeSO_4.NO \)

\( Pb(NO_3)_2 + 2HCl \rightarrow PbCl2 + 2HNO_3 \)

Reactions of Manganese Ion Mn+2

S.No. Experiment Observation

\( MnSO_4 + 2NaOH \rightarrow Mn(OH)_2 + Na_2SO_4 \)
