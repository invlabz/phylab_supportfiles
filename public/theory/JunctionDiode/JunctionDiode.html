Aim:

To draw I, V characteristic curves of a fucntion diode.

Apparatus:

p-n junction diode, Regulated power suppply unit, a milliammeter of range 0 to 20 mA, a micro ammeter of range 0 - 100 micro A, a voltmeter of range 0 - 10V and another voltmeter of range 0 - 1V

Theory:

a. Forward bias:

When the P end lead of diode is connected to the positive terminal and n end lead is connected to the negative terminal of a battery, then the diode is said to be forward biased. The diode conducts the electricity if the applied potential is greater than the barrier potential. As the applied potential increases, the current strength increases.

b. Reverse bias:

When p end lead of the diode is connected to the negative terminal and n end lead is connected to positive terminal of the battery the diode is said to be reverse biased. The minority carriers constitute some flow of electricity. This current is the order of microamperes, and it is called leakage current.

Procedure:

a. Forward bais:

Connections are made as shown in the circuit diagram using voltmeter and ammeter with proper ranges. The resistance Rs is selected for the safe current through the diode.

The voltage across diode is kept at 0.1V. The corresponding milliammeter reading I is notedm the potential difference across the diode is increased in stepd of 0.1V and the corresponding milliammeter reading I are noted. 8 reading are taken and tabulated. A graph is drawn between the voltage and current. It is called the diode forward characteristic. The straight line part of curve is extrapolated to cut the voltage axis at a potential Vk. This gives the knee voltage.

The forward resistance of diode is calculated using the formula,

\( r_f = \frac{\bigtriangleup V }{\bigtriangleup I} \)

b. Reverse biase:

The connections are made as shown in the circuit diagram, selecting the instruments of proper range. The voltage across the diode is kept at 10V and the microammeter reading I is noted. The potential across the diode is increased in steps of 5V and the corresponding currents in micro amps are noted and tabulated. A graph is drawn between the potential difference and current.
