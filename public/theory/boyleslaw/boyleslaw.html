Aim:

(i)To verify the Boyle's law and
(ii) to determine the atmospheric pressure from \(h-\frac{1}{L}\) graph

Apparatus:

Boyle's Law:At a constant temperature the volume(v) of a given mass of a gas is inversely propotional to its presure(p)
\(\therefore \, V\alpha \frac{1}{P} \, or\, PV=constant. \)

Air is taken as expermental gas in this apparatus. The volume of air is propotional to the length of air column(L). Instead of proving PV=constant, it is sufficient to show PL=constant. If L1, L2 are the lengths of air column at pressures P1and P2, then P1L1 =P2L2.

A=cross section area of uniform tube(cm2)
l=Length of air column (cm)
P= \( H \pm h \) pressure of air column(cm of Hg)
H=Atmospheric pressure=76 cmof Hg
h=h1-h2=Difference in mercury level heights(cm)
h1 =Vertical height of the Hg level from groung to open end side(cm)
h2 =Vertical height of the Hg level from ground to closed end side(cm)

QUILL TUBE APPARATUS:

Description:

AB is glass tube of uniform cross section of diameter about 2mm and length about 1m. 0nd of the tube is closed such that an air column of length about 30cm is enclosed by the closed end and a mercury pellet of length about 15cm. By changing the position of the tube the pressure of the air column can be changed. This tube is fixed on a meter scale and this is fixed to clamp of a retort stand. By adjusting the clamp the orientation of the tube along wuth the scale in vertical plane can be changed.

Procedure:

The tube along with the scale is fixed vertical with its open end upwards. Measure the length of the Air column 'L' between the closed end and the mercury pellet. Measure the vertical height h1 and h2 of the two ends of the mercury pellet from the tube. (h1 or h2)=h the pressure difference. If H is the atmospheric pressure, the pressure on air column is P=(H+h). The product of P and L is calculated. the experiment is repeated by keeping the tube in different positions with the help of the clamp. In each position the length of the air column is measured. The vertical heights h1 and h2 of the end of the mercury pellet are measure (h1 or h2) is the pressure difference. If the open end is in downward position the preassure the pressure on the air column is P=H-h. Thus pressure are calculated and the products P x L are calculated. The readings are tabulated.

h-1/L graph:

Draw a graph taking h value along X-axis and 1/L values along Y-axis. The graph is a straight line and the intercept on the X-axis, i. e. , OB gives the atmospheric pressure.

Result:

  1. The temperature should be constant.
  2. The tube should not be touched by the hand.
  3. For every change of position of the reservior, wait for some time and take readings
  4. The tube should be of uniform cross section.
